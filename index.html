Skin Allergy Treatment

Pulmonary Function Test (PFT)

A vital diagnostic tool for assessing lung health. Measures lung capacity, airflow, and gas exchange efficiency. Crucial in diagnosing respiratory conditions and monitoring treatment effectiveness.

Tracheostomy

Tracheostomy is a surgical procedure where an incision is made in the neck to create a direct airway through the trachea. It helps in managing breathing difficulties and is often used in emergency situations or for long-term ventilator support

Pneumonectomy

Pneumonectomy: A complex surgical procedure involving the removal of an entire lung to treat severe lung diseases such as cancer or infections. It aims to improve respiratory function and overall health, but requires extensive postoperative care and rehabilitation.

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) Treatment

A progressive lung condition characterized by airflow limitation, cough, and shortness of breath. It mainly includes chronic bronchitis and emphysema.

Skin Allergies

Effective skin allergy treatment requires proper diagnosis and personalized care. Consult a dermatologist for expert guidance on managing your specific condition.

Lung Surgery

A surgical procedure performed to treat various lung conditions, including lung cancer, infections, and lung diseases. It may involve removing tumors, repairing damaged lung tissue, or transplanting a healthy lung. Postoperative care and rehabilitation are crucial for recovery and optimal lung function.

Decortication

A surgical procedure involving the removal of fibrous tissue or membranes that restrict lung expansion. It aims to improve breathing by restoring lung elasticity and function. It's commonly performed to treat conditions like pleural effusion, empyema, or lung fibrosis.

Cough Treatment

Various approaches exist for managing cough, depending on the underlying cause. Options include over-the-counter cough suppressants, lozenges, humidifiers, staying hydrated, avoiding irritants, and addressing the root condition through targeted medications or therapy.

Tuberculosis (TB) Treatment

TB is a bacterial infection affecting the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. Treatment typically involves a combination of antibiotics for 6 to 9 months to eliminate the bacteria. Adequate medication adherence is crucial to ensure successful cure and prevent drug resistance

Allergy Treatment

Approaches to manage allergic reactions caused by various triggers. It includes avoidance strategies, medications like antihistamines, immunotherapy, and lifestyle modifications to minimize exposure and alleviate symptoms for improved quality of life.

Lung Transplant

A life-saving procedure where a diseased lung is replaced with a healthy lung from a deceased donor. It offers hope for those with end-stage lung disease, providing improved quality of life and long-term survival. However, it requires extensive evaluation, waiting for a suitable donor, and lifelong immunosuppressive.

Pneumonia Treatment

Involves antibiotics to target the specific cause, rest, fluid intake, and fever management. Severe cases may require hospitalization for oxygen therapy and supportive care. Early diagnosis and prompt treatment are crucial for a successful recovery.

Respiratory Conditions

A range of disorders affecting the respiratory system, including asthma, COPD, pneumonia, and lung cancer. They cause breathing difficulties and may require treatment with medications, therapies, and lifestyle modifications for optimal respiratory health.

Chronic Skin Allergy

A persistent allergic reaction affecting the skin, causing itching, redness, and rashes. Common triggers include certain foods, medications, or environmental factors. Management involves identifying allergens, avoiding triggers, and using topical treatments or antihistamines for symptom relief.
